Important Things for General Engineering

Key Points

  • A minimum of 30 credits (7.5 courses) in topic-specific engineering electives.
  • A minimum of 12 credits (3 courses) must start with the code ENG-UH.
  • To verify course eligibility, contact Prof. Khaled Shahin, the ABET accreditation coordinator (kos3@nyu.edu).
  • Graduate-level courses are an option but require permission from the General Engineering Dean, Prof. Margaret Julias (margaret.julias@nyu.edu).
  • Graduate-level courses are 3-credit courses; taking more than 2 of these may set you back in acquiring the 30 credits.

A Guide to Taking Graduate-Level Courses While Studying Abroad

Helpful steps for General Engineering students navigating graduate courses abroad.

1. Securing Approvals

- Seek permission from the General Engineering Dean (e.g., Prof. Margaret Julias at NYUAD).

- Contact the graduate school or department offering your desired courses to confirm availability and registration details.

Tip: Prepare a list of potential courses in advance, as not all options may be available.

2. Course Eligibility and Requirements

- Ensure graduate courses are listed under NYU Tandon and taught by engineering faculty to count towards your degree.

- Consult Prof. Khaled Shahin for questions about eligibility and ABET requirements.

3. Credit Load Management

- Limit yourself to no more than two graduate courses, as they are project-intensive and time-consuming.

- Avoid overloading to ensure you meet the 30-credit (7.5 course) requirement for topic-specific engineering electives.

4. Building a Backup Plan

- Prepare extra course options in case some are unavailable.

- Discuss alternatives with deans and academic advisors early in the process.

5. Time Management Tips

- Factor in the project intensiveness of graduate courses when planning your schedule.

- Use a calendar to track important deadlines for approvals, registration, and study abroad applications.

6. Leverage Faculty and Peer Advice

- Stay in touch with professors and deans for guidance and clarification throughout the process.

- Seek insights and tips from peers who have previously taken graduate courses.
