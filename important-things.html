Important Things for General Engineering

Key Points

  • A minimum of 30 credits (7.5 courses) in topic-specific engineering electives.
  • A minimum of 12 credits (3 courses) must start with the code ENG-UH.
  • To verify course eligibility, contact Prof. Khaled Shahin, the ABET accreditation coordinator (kos3@nyu.edu).
  • Graduate-level courses are an option but require permission from the General Engineering Dean, Prof. Margaret Julias (margaret.julias@nyu.edu).
  • Graduate-level courses are 3-credit courses; taking more than 2 of these may set you back in acquiring the 30 credits.

Guide to Taking Graduate-Level Courses While Studying Abroad

A concise guide for General Engineering students to navigate graduate courses during study abroad.

  1. Approvals and Permissions:
    • Secure permission from the General Engineering Dean (e.g., Prof. Margaret Julias at NYUAD).
    • Contact the graduate school or department offering the courses to confirm availability and registration details.
    Tip: Prepare a list of potential courses, as not all options may be approved.
  2. Course Eligibility:
    • Ensure courses are listed under NYU Tandon and taught by engineering faculty.
    • Verify eligibility with Prof. Khaled Shahin for ABET requirements.
  3. Manage Credit Load:
    • Limit yourself to no more than two graduate courses due to their project-intensive nature.
    • Ensure balance to meet elective credit requirements.
  4. Build a Backup Plan:
    • Prepare additional course options in case some are unavailable.
    • Discuss alternatives with deans or advisors.
  5. Organise and Plan Ahead:
    • Track deadlines for approvals, registration, and study abroad applications using a calendar.
    • Seek advice from professors and experienced peers.

Graduate-level courses can enrich your degree. Plan carefully, stay proactive, and make the most of this opportunity!
